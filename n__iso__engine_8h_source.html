626/* Per-frame callback that returns the screen-space (dx, dy) offset
627 * which the consumer has added to its world camera AFTER the iso
628 * engine's `cam_px / cam_py` were sampled (e.g. a temporal-AA
629 * sub-pixel jitter applied on top of the camera snap). The engine
630 * subtracts this offset from the endpoints of every decorative line
631 * draw (cliff-base edge lines in `_draw_segment_sides`; top-face
632 * edges, vertical corner lines, and underside borders in
633 * `_draw_height_borders`) so those lines stay pixel-stable across
634 * frames while filled polygon faces and bitmap sprites continue to
635 * inherit the jitter (which is what drives the temporal-AA averaging
636 * on bitmap content). Without the compensation, thin anti-aliased
637 * lines hit a different sub-pixel position each frame; the
638 * rasterizer's AA coverage flickers; any temporal blend downstream
639 * (TAA's history-weight composite) then averages two slightly
640 * offset semi-transparent lines into visible grey/white pixels along
641 * the line.
642 *
643 * Default NULL = no compensation. Consumers without a jitter source
644 * pay nothing. The callback is invoked per-tile during
645 * `iso_map_draw` (potentially hundreds of times per frame), so it
646 * must be O(1): typically returns the cached jitter from a
647 * `_begin_frame` call elsewhere. Returning (0, 0) is the "no
648 * compensation this frame" signal and is the expected steady state
649 * when the jitter source is off (e.g. camera stationary, TAA
650 * disabled). */
651typedef void (*IsoLineJitterCompensationFn)(float* out_dx, float* out_dy);
652void iso_set_line_jitter_compensation(IsoLineJitterCompensationFn fn);
653void iso_apply_line_jitter_compensation(float* out_dx, float* out_dy);
